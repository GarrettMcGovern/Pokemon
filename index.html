What is the Pokédex?

The Pokédex is an electronic device in the Pokémon series that records data about all Pokémon a trainer encounters or catches. It acts as an encyclopedia, providing each Pokémon’s name, type, height and weight, abilities, evolutions, and descriptions of its behaviors and characteristics. Its purpose is to help trainers catch Pokémon and learn more about them.

Pokédex Origin

The origin of the Pokédex in the video games comes from Professor Oak, who gives the player a Pokédex at the start of their journey to help document every Pokémon in the region and guide them along the way. In the game, the Pokédex allows players to see which Pokémon exist in the region, automatically updates entries when they encounter or catch Pokémon, and provides additional information once a Pokémon is unlocked or registered.

Pokédex types

There are several types of Pokédexes. Some are divided by region—these include the nine regions: Kanto, Johto, Hoenn, Sinnoh, Unova, Kalos, Alola, Galar, and Paldea. There is also the National Pokédex, which includes Pokémon from all regions and is typically unlocked after completing the regional Pokédex. In addition, there are special Pokédexes that record different forms and statistics of Pokémon. These include shiny forms, Mega Evolutions, Gigantamax forms, and entries sorted by attributes such as size or power.
